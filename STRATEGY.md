# StarCraft II Strategy: Economy vs. Army in Practice

The economy-vs-army tension described in [`PRINCIPLES.md`](PRINCIPLES.md) is the
axis that most strategies live on. This document places common strategy
archetypes on that axis, explains **how to detect** each from scouting, and
gives the **counter** for each.

The single most useful skill is reading *where on the economy–army spectrum the
opponent has committed*, then positioning yourself to punish the extreme they
chose.

## The spectrum

From all-army to all-economy:

```
  ALL-IN / CHEESE   TIMING ATTACK   STANDARD MACRO   GREEDY / ECO   TURTLE
  (no economy)      (delayed eco)   (balanced)       (light army)   (defense+tech)
  strong very early ......................................... strong late
```

The rough rock-paper-scissors:

- **Aggression beats greed** — an all-in or timing hits before a greedy economy
  can pay off.
- **Greed beats standard** — a bigger economy out-produces a balanced player
  over time.
- **Standard/defense beats aggression** — a prepared, balanced defender survives
  the all-in and then wins on the attacker's dead economy.

So the counter to any strategy is usually to move *one notch toward the side
that beats it*: play safer against aggression, play more aggressively against
greed.

## Archetypes: choice, detection, counter

### 1. Cheese / all-in (all army, no economy)

- **The choice:** spend everything on early army to end the game before economy
  matters. Proxy rushes, cannon rush, worker rushes, early all-in busts. If it
  fails, they have no economy and lose.
- **Detect it by what's *missing*:** no natural expansion, few or no workers
  added, early gas for a rush unit, an aggressive scout that hides their base,
  or buildings placed near *your* base (proxies). A base with no expansion and
  low worker count this early is almost always committing to aggression.
- **Counter:** scout early so you're not surprised; wall your ramp; pull workers
  and add static defense (bunker / spine / photon) to hold. You don't need to
  win the fight — you need to *not die*. If you survive, their economy is gone
  and you win by simply expanding and playing on.

### 2. Timing attack / one- or two-base all-in (army-heavy, delayed economy)

- **The choice:** take a small economy, pour the rest into army/tech/upgrades,
  and hit a specific **power spike** (see the timing section in `PRINCIPLES.md`)
  before the opponent's defenses or economy are ready.
- **Detect it by:** delayed or missing expansion relative to normal, extra
  production buildings, early upgrades or a key tech rushed, gas taken fast, and
  army massing / moving out at a specific time. Army supply spiking while worker
  count stays flat is the tell.
- **Counter:** don't over-expand into it — hold back economy until you've
  scouted safety. Match the composition, defend at your ramp/choke with
  defender's advantage, and hold the timing. Once it breaks, their economy is
  behind, so expand and punish the window while they rebuild.

### 3. Standard macro (balanced)

- **The choice:** expand on a normal timing while keeping enough army to be safe
  — the default "play the game straight" approach.
- **Detect it by:** natural taken around the standard timing, steady worker and
  army growth together, a reactive rather than committed build.
- **Counter:** there's no free punish — win on execution. Look for an edge in
  upgrades, composition matchup, a small timing where you spike first, or
  slightly greedier economy if you scout that they're not threatening. This is
  the "out-macro and out-micro them" matchup.

### 4. Greedy / fast-expand / economic (economy-heavy, light army)

- **The choice:** extra expansions or fast tech with minimal army, betting on a
  bigger economy later. Strong ceiling, but a wide vulnerability window *now*.
- **Detect it by:** an early third (or fast second), low army supply, few
  production buildings, gas-heavy tech structures, and static defense standing in
  for units. Lots of bases + few units = greed.
- **Counter:** punish it before it pays off. Apply pressure or run a timing
  attack into the window where their army is thin — force them to pull workers or
  lose a base. If you can't punish (no map access, they defended the window),
  then you must match their economy or you lose the long game.

### 5. Turtle / defensive tech (economy behind static defense, teching to a
strong late-game)

- **The choice:** heavy static defense and tech toward a powerful late-game army
  (splash, air, capital units), trading map control and early tempo for a strong
  finish.
- **Detect it by:** rings of static defense, few units leaving home, gas
  prioritized for high tech, and no interest in taking the middle of the map.
- **Counter:** take the rest of the map — out-expand them so your economy dwarfs
  theirs. Don't attack into static defense. Either hit *before* their key tech
  completes, or tech to your own counter to their late-game composition. Time is
  on the side of whoever has more bases, so make sure that's you.

## Reading tech: detection and counters

Beyond *how much* economy and army the opponent has, you need to read *what tech
they're going for*. Tech decides which units and abilities appear, and many games
are lost by being unprepared for a tech you could have scouted. The rule: **the
tech building is visible before the units are** — react to the building, not the
army that arrives too late.

### Detecting a tech direction

- **Read the tech building, not the unit.** Tech structures (and their add-ons)
  telegraph the units to come — the building appears minutes before the army
  does. Seeing the building is your warning.
- **Gas is the tell.** Fast or heavy gas means tech or a gas-heavy unit is
  coming. Pure-economy openings take gas late; early double-gas signals a tech
  play.
- **Cloak / burrow / air need a *specific* answer.** These are the punishing
  techs — if scouting hints at them (the enabling tech building, or gas with no
  visible army), assume the worst and prepare detection or anti-air *before* it
  lands.
- **What's missing tells you too.** A tech building with little army = teching at
  the cost of current strength (a vulnerability window now). No tech building on
  heavy gas = the gas is banked for a rush unit about to appear.

### The two tech mistakes and their counters

- **Tech rush (teching too fast, thin army):** they gave up current strength for
  a later spike. *Counter:* apply pressure or hit a timing during the window
  before the tech comes online — punish the thin army, or force them to pull off
  teching to defend.
- **Tech-blind (no answer to what you scout):** getting surprised by cloak, air,
  or splash you could have seen coming. *Counter (for you):* tech reactively —
  the moment you read the enabling building, start your answer (detection,
  anti-air, splash) so it's ready when their units arrive.

### Countering by teching yourself

Sometimes the right response to a composition isn't more of the same units but a
**tech switch** — teching to a hard counter (detection vs. cloak, anti-air vs.
air, splash vs. massed light, a higher-tier unit vs. their tier). Remember it
costs tempo and opens a window while the switch comes online, so cover that
window with what you already have.

## Detection cheat-sheet

Scout for these signals and infer the economy–army commitment:

- **Base count vs. worker count vs. army supply.** The ratio places them on the
  spectrum. Many bases + few units = greed; one base + many units = aggression.
- **Expansion timing.** Earlier than standard = greedy; later or absent = an
  all-in or timing.
- **Gas timing and count.** Early/heavy gas = rushing tech or a gas-heavy unit
  (a rush or tech play), not pure economy.
- **Production count.** Many production buildings on a small economy = an army
  commitment (timing/all-in).
- **Proxies and positioning.** Buildings toward your base, or an army moving out
  early, signal aggression.
- **Tech buildings and add-ons.** They telegraph the units and abilities coming
  minutes ahead — react to the building, not the army. Watch especially for the
  enablers of cloak, burrow, and air.
- **What's *missing*.** No expansion → aggression. No army → greed. The absence
  is often the loudest signal.

## The general counter procedure

1. **Scout** to locate the opponent on the economy–army spectrum.
2. **Classify** the archetype from the signals above (and re-scout, since they
   transition).
3. **Move one notch toward the side that beats it:** safer and more defensive
   against aggression; more economic or aggressive against greed.
4. **When unsure, err toward safety.** Dying to an unscouted all-in loses
   instantly; being slightly behind on economy loses slowly and is recoverable.
5. **Punish the window.** Every choice on the spectrum opens a window — the
   greedy player is weak now, the aggressive player is weak later. Hit the window
   their choice created.
